A central interface to the R quant stack. Four walk-forward workflows share one grammar (config + data → run_*() → *_results) and one set of engines, orchestrating twelve specialist packages behind a single, versionable, point-in-time-safe API.

Characteristic portfolios run_port_backtest()

Realistic, cost-aware, constraint-aware backtesting of any signal, the same engine that later deploys the final book.

Engines

  • set_portfolio_weights()★ one weighting engine, reused verbatim by signal blending (W3)
  • classify_investment_universe()★ same eligibility grammar used for stocks here and signals in W2
  • estimate_covariance_matrix()sample · PCA · shrinkage, active or total returns

Features

  • 9 construction methods: EW · SW · CW · CS · HRP · MMAF built in-house; RP with tilts & constraints; MVO with box/group/concentration constraints, custom objectives, random/grid/DE search, resampling & shrinkage
  • Transaction costs (direct + dynamic market impact) with a full auditable trade log
  • Liquidity floors & caps, turnover buffer zones, user-defined AND/OR eligibility rules
  • Expected-return-score shrinkage & combination; benchmark-relative everything
  • Cohorts: aggregate style portfolios and compare them as a group

Signal selection run_ss_backtest()

Which signals are real? Disciplined, walk-forward inference on CAPM alpha, taming the factor zoo.

Engines

  • summarize_performance()★ per-signal CAPM metrics; also powers target creation (stage 0)
  • fit_frequentist_hierarchical_model()partial pooling via lme4 / lmerTest
  • fit_bayesian_hierarchical_model()brms / Stan MCMC, parallel chains via future

Features

  • Multiple-testing control: FWER (Bonferroni, Holm) and FDR (BH, BY)
  • No-pooled OLS or partial-pooled hierarchical CAPM,4 theme-level specifications
  • Bayesian shrinkage with user priors or data-informed priors derived from an exogenous global dataset
  • Hypothesis testing applied walk-forward, expanding or rolling, at every rebalance
  • Theme representativeness: no economic theme silently drops out

3

Signal blending run_sb_backtest()

Combine the selected signals into one score per stock: heuristics to deep learning behind one interface, one tuning API, one walk-forward scheme.

Engines

  • hyper_tune()grid · random · Bayesian optimization, parallel via furrr/doFuture
  • fit_sb_model()one dispatcher over 12 blending algorithms
  • set_portfolio_weights()★ heuristic blenders treat signals as assets: the W1 engine again, enabling factor timing

Features

  • ML: OLS · glmnet · random forest · XGBoost · Keras/TensorFlow NN, with tunable hyperparameters, custom objectives (Huber, pinball and more), early stopping, many eval metrics
  • Heuristics: EW · SW · RP · HRP · MVO · MMAF · custom weights over the signal universe
  • Interpretability end-to-end: global-surrogate feature importance + explain_prediction()
  • Meta-ensembles (sb_metabacktest): blend the blenders: backtest processes, not signals. Second-level thinking: a system, not a backtest

Deployment run_port_backtest(sb_results)

The out-of-sample blended score feeds straight back into the W1 engine, now with the full machinery of constraints, costs and audit trail, producing the final deployable book. The loop closes.

Engines

  • set_portfolio_weights()★ third appearance: one engine from research to production

Features

  • Per-stock exp_ret_score from the blender drives final weights
  • Concentration, turnover & liquidity constraints; signal shrinkage; transaction costs; trade log
  • Every result object carries its full workflow history and a backtest_identifier: reproducible, comparable, auditable

Built like software, not like a script (the Ops in FactorOps)

Reproducible:renv lockfile pins all 227 dependencies; every release ships tarball + lockfile
Versionable:S4 objects carry their full workflow history; named configs propagate into every result
Modular:fluent create_*_config() |> add_*() pipelines; pieces snap together and swap
Auditable:trade logs, workflow slots, backtest identifiers; any number traces to its inputs
Parallelizable:future / furrr / doFuture across tuning, MCMC and resampling; one plan() call
Tested & checked:73 test files; R CMD check --as-cran green on Ubuntu & Windows, every push
Deployable:tag-triggered releases gated on a passing check; docs auto-published via pkgdown
Point-in-time safe:signals at t use only information available at t; guardrails against look-ahead & survivorship bias
